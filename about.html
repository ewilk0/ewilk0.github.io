Who am I?

 

Hi, I'm Ethan Wilk, and I created this blog to post my  ideas and insight regarding various financial markets as well as update my efforts to promote environmental and social work through my 501(c)(3) non-profit organization.

 

About Me

 

I was born in New Jersey and moved to Scottsdale, Arizona when I was 3.

 

Current Work

 

I actively work as Founder and Director of Operations at Argonautica Programming (Argo-P), to which more information is given in the "Past Work" section of this page.

 

At the moment, I'm working with an economics professor and a local gold mining company to investigate the driving forces of the gold market, specifically those relating to stock-to-flow models and proportionate scarcity of money.

 

Additionally, I'm currently developing a second website to assemble all the quantitative models and engines discussed on this blog and represent their performance in real-time.

 

Finally, I'm working with a peer to develop a cryptocurrency-based algorithmic hedge fund that promotes decentralization in the finance community.

 

Past Work

 

A large portion of my sophomore and junior years in high school have been occupied by the development of Argo-P, a non-profit I started with a few peers. Continuing to raise funds to this day, we've been able to raise enough money to donate Google Chromebooks to an entire class of children in need around our local community. In addition, the non-profit has successfully worked with the World Wild Fund to reduce illegal fishing in the Asian Pacific using blockchain-based RFID chips in tuna fish.

 

Last summer, I took on an internship at a local startup company Systems Imagination, where I modeled convolutional neural networks to improve performance of big data sorting for the company's database. That same summer, I was accepted into LaunchX's entrepreneurial program at Northwestern University.

 

That same summer following my summer year of high school, I was able to work with another local company Kronos Consulting by updating their MySQL database and was also able to contribute to the company's discussions regarding business management. Similar to the work I contributed at Systems Imagination, this job enabled me to gain first-hand insight into the inner workings of technology-based companies, and their efforts to improve the environment outside of business.

 

Before my sophomore year of high school, I co-founded the entrepreneurship club at my high school with a peer. With the mission to spread our passion of investing and the entrepreneurial spirit, we contacted the LaunchX foundation to enhance the opportunities and outreach for our members. That same year, the entrepreneurship club became the most popular club at the school, as our members reached maximum acceptable capacity. Since then, the club has produced two functioning companies, one of which I contributed to heavily, now known as ResuME.
