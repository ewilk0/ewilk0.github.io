About Me

Who are you?

 

My name is Ethan Wilk and I'm a 17-year old from Scottsdale, Arizona.

 

What is this?

 

This is my personal blog where I post info about my projects as they occur. Occasionally, in the spirit of an upcoming event (e.g. March Madness, 2020 Presidential Elections), I may write some posts about applying advanced statistical models to real-world outcome prediction because I think it's really cool.

 

What do you do?

 

A couple things, but the TL;DR is that I love to use my unique abilities to inspire those around me and foster large-scale change, improving the community as a whole.

 

Nonprofit work

 

I founded and now run a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization that promotes marine sustainability through the novel use of blockchain technology. I strongly recommend you check out the website at https://www.thexeniaproject.org for more information, as we're always looking for more volunteers!

 

Speaking of volunteers, my organization has welcomed over 500 of them in the past year, and our technology is now being used to assist the World Wildlife Fund with their own marine projects in Australia. Exciting stuff! The nonprofit also won a National Earth Day Award in April, and I plan to use the funds to fuel the organization's expansion!

 

Track & Field

 

Outside the nonprofit, I'm the captain of my school's varsity track team. I learned of the importance of physical health in high school, and I now rank as the fastest sprinter in my league's state-wide division, allowing me to gain recruitment offers to CalTech and UChicago.

 

As captain, I work to function as a positive role model for younger kids around my community. To this end, I regularly train with less fortunate students at local tracks and love to connect with the community that built me up to the individual I am today.
