FINANCE & BUSINESS

*This is a copy of the article I wrote for the Young Investors Society after winning 2nd place in their Global Stock Pitch Competition, sponsored by Investors Business Daily*

 

---

 

The outbreak of COVID-19 has led to one of the most disastrous market shakeouts in world history. However, as a sound investor, we must be confident in the market’s ability to recover, leaving the question on everyone’s mind as when is the bottom?

 

This article will share a brief guide to determining a stock’s bottom based on insider trading via Form 4 filings. In the words of Peter Lynch, “insiders might sell their shares for any number of reasons, but they buy them for only one: they think the price will rise.”

 

• Navigate to https://www.insidertracking.com/, a site that publishes Form 4 filings as the SEC releases them.

 

• Navigate to the “Issuers with Net Buying” section of the home page, a compilation of companies witnessing mass long positions.

 

• Compile a brief list of promising, blue-chip companies from that list. Note: Avoid companies that have recently had their IPOs.

 

• From the list, find the companies that have a Relative Strength Index (RSI) lower than that of the S&P 500.

 

• Note: The RSI is a simple indicator to measure how oversold or overbought a stock is, and if a stock’s RSI is below the market, it has witnessed more selling action, giving it more room for potential gain.

 

For my own list, I found JPMorgan Chase & Co. ($JPM), SunPower ($SPWR), and Simply Good Foods Co. ($SMPL) to be some promising stocks. Keros Therapeutics ($KROS) had recently had its IPO, and electroCore ($ECOR) was overbought relative to the market, making the two invalid picks for this guide. Now, unleash your inner Marc Faber and find some stock picks!
